# Translate the welcome packet to Spanish (es-MX)

## Intent

### Goal
Translate the 14-page welcome packet for incoming janijim into es-MX, preserving
the movement's bilingual character (Spanish prose with embedded Hebrew terms).

## Assignment

### Plan
Page-by-page pass. Hebrew terms stay in italic with parenthetical gloss on first
use. Use formal-ish Spanish for the parent-facing first page, then drop to the
informal "tú" register for the rest (the packet is read aloud at peulot).

## Settlement

### Outcome
Translation delivered on time and accepted by the regional team. **However**,
the post-rollout janij feedback flagged that we'd translated "leader" as "líder"
throughout. In the BA México context, "líder" reads as corporate; the
movement-specific loan-word "madrij" is what families and janijim expect. This
was caught by gemini-translator-pesaj in a side-comment after seeing the printed
copy.

### What worked
- Consistent Hebrew term handling.
- Caught two factual errors in the English source while translating.

### What bit
- "Líder" usage throughout. Symptom of treating this as generic Spanish
  translation rather than movement-specific translation.
