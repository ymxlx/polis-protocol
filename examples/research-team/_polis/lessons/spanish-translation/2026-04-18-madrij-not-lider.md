# Lesson: Spanish translations for BA México need movement vocabulary, not Hispanic-corporate Spanish

When translating youth-movement content for the BA México audience, the
Hispanic-Spanish word "líder" reads as corporate or hierarchical; the loan-word
"madrij" (Hebrew for guide, used in the movement) is the right choice and is
what janijim and parents expect. This applied across welcome packets, parent
calls, and event invites in our March rollout, and post-feedback flagged it
as the single most jarring vocabulary choice.

Same shape applies to: `janij` (not "estudiante" or "miembro"), `peulá` (not
"actividad"), `tnuá` (not "movimiento juvenil" when speaking inside the
movement).

**Routing consequence**: future `spanish-translation` contracts for any BA México
property should default to gemini-translator-pesaj (vocabulary-strong) or
require movement-aware review when claude-research-pesaj claims them. After
this lesson and one more high-quality settlement, Gemini overtook Claude on the
`spanish-translation` tag in `routing_stats.yml`.

**Quality impact**: 4 — changed who gets routed on this tag and why.
